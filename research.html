Cutting-Edge Technologies of Interest

CRISPR Base Editing 2.0

Advanced base editors (ABEs and CBEs) enabling precise single-nucleotide changes without double-strand breaks, revolutionizing gene therapy with minimal off-target effects.

Applications: Sickle cell anemia treatment, cancer immunotherapy, agricultural crop improvement

Prime Editing

Search-and-replace genome editing without double-stranded DNA breaks or donor templates, enabling insertions, deletions, and base-to-base conversions.

Applications: Correcting pathogenic mutations, modeling genetic diseases, functional genomics

Single-Cell Multi-Omics

Simultaneous profiling of genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics at single-cell resolution, revealing cellular heterogeneity and disease mechanisms.

Applications: Cancer research, immunology, developmental biology, neuroscience

Cell-Free Protein Synthesis

In vitro protein production systems bypassing cellular machinery, enabling rapid prototyping of proteins and therapeutic antibodies.

Applications: Rapid vaccine development, toxic protein production, point-of-care diagnostics

Organoid Technology

3D organ-like structures grown from stem cells, mimicking organ functionality for drug testing and disease modeling.

Applications: Drug screening, personalized medicine, regenerative therapy, disease modeling

Nanopore Sequencing

Real-time, long-read DNA/RNA sequencing technology enabling direct detection of epigenetic modifications without amplification.

Applications: Rapid pathogen identification, structural variant detection, field-deployable diagnostics
